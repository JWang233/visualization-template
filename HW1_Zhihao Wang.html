      From above plots, all of the three variables (CRIME, HOVAL, and INC) do not strictly follow the normal distributions. First, the histograms show that the distribution of the variable CRIME is relatively even, compared with the other two variables. Thus, CRIME is not normally distributed. Although the distributions of HOVAL and INC in histograms look like a normal distribution, the QQ plots show that both of them have many outliers which are far away from the theoretical quantiles (shown as the straight lines). Thus, HOVAL and INC do not follow the assumption of normal distribution. In addition, from the boxplots, CRIME does not have any outlier sample while HOVAL and INC have several outliers beyond the upper bounds. To obtian more accurate results, these outliers should be filtered out.

      From the above plots, there exists a significantly strong non-linear relationship between CRIME and INC. Their correlation is -0.7 with the p-value of 0.001, and the fitted curve is shown as red in the bivariate scatter plot. Because of this strong relationship, CRIME and HOVAL are not suitable for being considered as independent variables together in order to fit non-linear regression models.

5. Residual Analysis¶

In [10]:
crime.resid = crime.lm$residuals # obtain residuals 

# plot 
par(mfrow=c(2,2), pty='s')

# residuals vs. independent variables 
plot(columbus.df$HOVAL, crime.resid, xlab='HOVAL', ylab='Residuals')
abline(h=0)
plot(columbus.df$INC, crime.resid, xlab='INC', ylab='Residuals')
abline(h=0)

# histogram and QQ plot
hist(crime.resid, xlab='Residuals')
qqnorm(crime.resid, ylab='Residuals')
qqline(crime.resid)

par(mfrow=c(1,1))

      The linear model is not fitted well. From the model summary table, the adjusted $R^2$ is only 0.53, which indicates a weak relationships between the dependent and independent variables. Additionally, the p-value of the HOVAL is about 0.1, greater than 0.05, which means it is not a statistically significant relationship between them. Second, the standard deviation of the residuals in this linear model is about 11.43 on 46 DOF. While it is not a very large value, it still reflect the great variance of the fitted and the original values. Third, from the histogram and the QQ plot, most residuals are concentrated around 0, and most samples lie on the theoritical line of the normal distribtion. Thus, we can say the residuals are normally distributed and follow the assumpltion of the linear regression. Given above contexts, while the residuals are normally distributed, the great variance and $R^2$ make it difficult to reject the null hypothesis which assumes there is no relationship between the dependent variable CRIME and the independent variables, HOVAL and INC.
